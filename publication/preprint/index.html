Building Information Modelling (BIM) and Bigdata integration Aided Sustainable Building management: a bibliometric led view

Tzuhui Wu, Zhen Liu
January 0001
Custom Link

Abstract

The Architectural, Engineering and Construction (AEC) industry has entered the era of big data. Building information management allows for multidisciplinary information and data generated throughout the building lifecycle to achieve sustainbility. However, the construction industry has not yet completely benefitted by big data. Few studies concentrated the integration of BIM and big data in the field of sustainable building management. The predecessors have a monotonous perspective in identifying the development of BIM and big data application in SBM. Therefore, this paper aims to critically analyze existing studies that combines BIM and big data domains from various bibliometric perspectives in the fields of SBM to identify the aspects where additional efforts are required and distinguish future directions. This paper adopts a mixed method of quantitative and qualitative analysis, including bibliometric analysis and knowledge mapping, which provides a macro-overview and reveals deeper insights into the research status and development trends of BIM and Big data integration (BBi)-aided SBM from multiple bibliometric perspectives (i.e., changes in the number of publications, changes in journal citations, keyword evolution, and development in thematic maps). The results indicate that the current studies on BBi-aided SBM mainly focused on data integration and interoperability in collaboration, development of information technologies and emerging technologies, data analysis and presentation, and green building & sustainability assessment. Moreover, the analysis of three time-slice phases (i.e., year 2010-2014, year 2015-2018, and year 2019-2021) over the past 12 years (year 2010 to 2021) indicates that the studies on BBi-aided SBM have been expanded from the application of BIM in construction projects to the integration and interoperability of BIM with information technology, the integration of virtual models with physical buildings, and sustainable management throughout the building lifecycle stages. Finally, key research gaps and new directions for future works were identified that future research could pay more attention to data integration and model interoperability across the building lifecycle, model transferability in the application of technology, and a comprehensive sustainability assessment framework based on the whole building lifecycle stages.

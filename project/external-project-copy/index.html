The Beauty of Intangible Cultural Heritage - Guangzhou Tooth Carving

Jan 1, 0001
Follow

In a contemporary society where new technologies are constantly developing, many intangible cultural crafts are facing the dilemma of gradually their heritage. The main problems are that the crafts are complex and the economic benefits are slow in coming, so there are fewer and fewer inheritors; the inheritors are facing economic difficulties; some crafts only remain in the static protection of museums; the cultural and creative works are templated and mechanised, so the general public cannot feel the charm of the crafts; the general public lacks a personal understanding of intangible cultural crafts, etc. There is an urgent need for a new way to inherit intangible culture and to attract more people to learn more about it.

The co-design service platform integrates online and offline resources, providing services such as knowledge learing, craft learning, thematic creation, social interaction and merchandise purchase online, and offline carving activities created by craft inheritors in collaboration with non-traditional partners, the BOP community. Through the participation of public, more people will be attracted to learn and understand the culture of tooth carving, thus realising the heritage of tooth carving in Guangzhou.

The main products that work with the Co-design Service Platform are the APP and the mystery boxes. Users can book offline activities on the APP, working with other users of different professional backgrounds to create carvings. For those who are unable to participate in offline activities, the APP also provides an online event portal where users can purchase material kits and instructional videos to create their own work at home. The APP also provides certain free teaching videos and incorporates VR videos into the online teaching. In addition, the APP also has a social function that allows users to share their creation process and results. After the offline activities, the platform collects and selects the works created by the users, and invites tooth carving craftsmen to re-process them and make them into a series of themed mystery boxes. The mystery boxes will be sold on the APP.

The platform was a start-up project with a fund of RMB 200,000, which was self-funded by the team members. The subsequent reinvestment capital is RMB 3 million, of which 20% comes from the investment of the Folk Craft Museum, 80% is raised by the team members at a later stage, 50% of the self-financing fund comes from the rest of the cooperative investment and public fund donations, 20% is self-financed by the student team and 10% comes from online crowdfunding. The main profit point comes from the platform’s drawback income, with a payback period of about 2 years, and has good operation and profitability.
